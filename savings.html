Savings and MMDAs

Savings and MMDAs are easy ways to get interest on money. Some savings accounts are connected to checking accounts for easy access. When you put money into a savings account, you can earn interest on it, though not as much as a CD. However, you can get access to this money very frequently, and put money in/out. MMDAs are similar, but they may have higher APY and less access.

Real Rates

Here are some real Savings and MMDA rates from Ally Bank. The Ally Bank Online Savings Account has a 2.2% APY. There aren't any fees and you get to do 6 transactions. The Ally Bank Money Market Account's APY depends on daily balance, with 0.9% APY if you have $5,000 or less in your account, and 1% APY if you have $25,000 in your account. There are unlimited deposits and ATM withdrawals with it.

Why These Could Be Beneficial For You

If you are just starting out in savings and interest, these might be the best option for you. A Savings account is easy to open and a good way to get started as they have decent APY and let you get money in and out. MMDAs are also a good starting point as there are more ways to get money in and out.
